Slide 1 · Cold open (~15s)

Open cold, no intro. Read the line. Pause. Let it land. This is the only emotional beat you get before the audience decides whether to listen.

If asked "did this actually happen": the specific $5,000 scene is illustrative. The real-world failure mode is well documented — see the CoinDesk April 2026 piece where a malicious LLM router drained a user's wallet of ~$500K, and the May 2026 Bankr/Grok prompt-injection incident where ~$155–174K was drained from a Twitter-deployed agent before partial recovery.

Slide 2 · The new problem (~25s)

Frame: agents are toddlers with credit cards. They can transact, they cannot judge.

If asked about the headline numbers: sourced from the Chainalysis 2026 Crypto Crime Report — $3.4B stolen in 2025, and $104B moved through sanctioned wallets (a 694% YoY surge). The point is order of magnitude, not the decimal.

If asked "is anyone else naming this category": yes — CoinDesk (Apr 2026) framed it as a "hidden flaw" in agent-driven payments; TRM Labs published on autonomous-agent financial-crime risk. The industry is starting to talk about it. We're shipping the answer.

If asked "why hasn't this been solved": all existing tools (Chainalysis, TRM, Etherscan) were built for human analysts on a desk. None of them speak agent-native (structured verdict, pay-per-call, sub-second).

Slide 3 · Who feels the pain (~25s)

ICP: agent platform builders — teams of 5–50 engineers shipping agents that move USDC. Examples: Crew.ai builders, LangGraph teams, x402/Bazaar app developers, agentic-commerce startups (Skyfire-adjacent).

Why this ICP: they feel the pain today, they can integrate one API call, and they have no budget for Chainalysis. Short cycle, high logo count — ideal for post-hackathon validation.

Adjacent (mention if asked): crypto exchanges & wallet providers (bigger contracts), and RegTech/AML compliance teams.

Slide 4 · Ward-o, in one breath (~25s)

The line to memorize: "Ward-o is the seatbelt for agents that spend money." Don't say "API". Don't say "endpoint". Don't say "x402". The seatbelt metaphor does the work — it's automatic, it's invisible until you need it, and you'd never build a car without it.

What it actually is: one input (wallet address), one structured verdict (safe / risk_flag / insufficient_data), in seconds, for cents. The 12-year-old test: "you ask the agent a question, it tells you if the wallet is safe to pay."

Backup framing if "seatbelt" doesn't land: "Ward-o is a guardrail — it constrains the agent before the costly action, so the mistake never happens." Use this when the room is technical and wants to hear about prevention, not just recovery.

Slide 5 · Benefits (~20s)

Pitch the three words, then move. Time. Money. Trust. Don't read every bullet — the slide does that.

If pressed on "money saved": per Vendr marketplace data, Chainalysis enterprise contracts have a median of ~$200k/yr and a range of $25k–$300k. Ward-o pay-per-call lets a Maya-sized team get coverage for under $100/month.

If pressed on "trust kept": the real product is end-user willingness to keep the agent funded. Every sanctioned-address mistake collapses that trust permanently.

Slide 6 · The staircase (~20s)

The thesis: devs are the wedge, not the destination. Today the pain is felt by Maya. In 12 months it's felt by companies running agent fleets. In 2–3 years it's felt by every consumer app with agent checkout. In the far future it's grandma doing crypto through her ChatGPT-class assistant — and none of it works without a trust layer.

The one-line takeaway: "We start where the pain is felt today. We become the safety layer for the agent economy tomorrow."

If asked "when does each rung land": 12-month rung = once 2–3 major agent platforms ship payment SDKs (already happening with Crew/LangGraph). 2–3 year rung = once consumer agent checkout hits ~$1B GMV (Stripe-style adoption curve). Far future = depends on regulatory and UX maturity, but the building block is the same.

If asked about competitor response: Chainalysis can't pivot down-market — their pricing, sales cycle, and analyst-first UX are structural. By the time they ship an agent-native tier, the integration ecosystem will be wired around us.
