## What

Background vetter job that runs on a 12h schedule to keep the service registry healthy: probes active/probation services for price drift, auto-bumps prices when the real x402 price exceeds what's stored, discovers new CDP catalog candidates, and recomputes scores/status transitions.

## Files

- `src/vetter/run.ts` — main logic (`runVetter()`)
- `src/vetter/run_test.ts` — 5 unit tests covering all AC scenarios
- `scripts/vet.ts` — manual-trigger entry point (`deno task vet`)
- `.github/workflows/vetter.yml` — GHA cron (every 12h, `workflow_dispatch` for manual)
- `deno.json` — added `vet` task

## Config

No new env vars. Reads existing `DATABASE_URL` and `AGNIC_API_KEY` from the `deno-deploy` GHA environment. The `deno task vet` manual path also picks them from `.env`.

## Notes

- Price probe sends a plain GET with no payment header; handles both `amount` and `maxAmountRequired` in the 402 response body (implementations vary between CDP convention and the x402 spec).
- Safety ceiling is $0.10 USDC. Above that, the service goes to probation instead of auto-bumping — prevents silent runaway cost increases.
- Auto-bump factor is ×1.20 (20% buffer over the real price).
- Bumped prices are propagated back to `data/call_recipes.json` so `seed-registry.ts` doesn't undo them on the next seed.
- Discovery step calls the live CDP catalog; runs even when the DB is in no-op mode (offline), but inserts are silently skipped.
- When `DATABASE_URL` is unset, the vetter runs in no-op mode: probes and discovery still run, but no DB writes are issued and score recompute is a no-op.
